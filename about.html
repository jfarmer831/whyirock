Mission

To inspire young girls of color to reach their best selves, defy stereotypes, shatter glass ceilings, embrace their ethnicity and culture, and love themselves for who they are.

Our aim is to inspire, motivate, inform, and encourage young girls of color to appreciate their culture, promote positive self-esteem and body images, become familiar with women in Portland who can be positive mentors, and interrupt cycles that have plagued this demographic for years.

Young girls of color, and particularly Black/African-American girls have been overlooked and under-resourced for years resulting in over representation in the pipeline to prison epidemic, over representation in school disciplinary systems, over representation in commercially sexually trafficked scenarios, statistically higher percentages of negative body image perceptions, poor health, poor mental health, and so much more. One step we have taken to join in the fight to reversing this epidemic is hosting an annual 2 day empowerment conference.


WHY I ROCK CONFERENCE 2018

The Why I Rock Girls Conference is geared toward a very vulnerable population of at risk girls of color, and aims to improve health outcomes, interrupt negative narratives that continue to oppress and act as a prevention strategy to interrupt generational cycles of poverty for the 100+ girls of color in attendance as well as their parents and the African-American community at large. The conference consist of two full days of educational and inspirational events.
